1. Introduction
1.1 Purpose
This Software Requirements Specification (SRS) document defines the complete functional and non-functional requirements for an online car buying and selling platform targeting the Kuwaiti market. The platform — referred to hereinafter as the “System” — will enable individuals, dealers and showrooms to buy, sell, finance, inspect and service vehicles through a unified web portal and native mobile applications (iOS, Android and Huawei AppGallery).
The System shall provide a complete set of capabilities expected of a modern digital automotive marketplace operating in Kuwait, including concierge-style assisted selling, self-service classifieds, professional 71-point inspections, integrated financing with local banks, and add-on car services (wash, tinting, registration renewal, glass repair, tires, and health check).
This document is intended for the project sponsor, product owner, solution architects, software engineers (frontend and backend), QA engineers, designers, DevOps engineers, the security officer, and any third-party vendors providing integrations (banks, payment gateways, SMS providers, inspection partners, Ministry of Interior services, etc.).
1.2 Scope
The System covers the full online vehicle commerce lifecycle in Kuwait. It supports two complementary commercial models on a single platform:
Retailer model — for inventory owned or controlled by the operator, customers can complete an end-to-end digital purchase: reserve the car with a refundable deposit, secure financing and insurance online, sign the contract digitally, take home-delivery, and benefit from a money-back return window.
Marketplace model — for cars sold by certified dealers, registered showrooms and individual private sellers, the platform facilitates discovery, inquiries, valuations and assisted closing while the parties complete the sale through the conventional channels.
In addition, the System operates a dedicated B2B Dealer Auction Portal for wholesale trade between approved dealerships. The full functional scope is summarized below:
Browsing and searching a marketplace of pre-owned and new cars listed by platform-operated inventory, certified dealers, registered showrooms and individual private sellers.
Reserving an operator-owned vehicle with a refundable KWD 100 deposit and a 48-hour hold, followed by a guided 7-step purchase wizard (payment method, trade-in, add-ons, document upload, digital contract signing, delivery slot, confirmation).
Selling a car via three distinct journeys: (a) a Concierge Service with operator-led inspection, marketing and closing; (b) a Self-Service classified listing; and (c) an Instant Online Valuation with an optional guaranteed part-exchange or sell-only offer.
Professional 71-point vehicle inspection conducted in-house or through a certified third-party inspection partner, with structured exterior, mechanical, electronic, interior and test-drive checks, plus 25+ HD photos, a 360° walkaround and a 1–2 minute video.
Loan pre-qualification (soft check) and full financing through partner banks and financial institutions, including a finance calculator and a &apos;search by monthly payment&apos; filter.
One-click insurance activation at purchase through partner insurers, with third-party and comprehensive coverage options.
Home delivery scheduling with date/time slots, real-time driver GPS tracking, and a digital handover checklist.
A 3-day / 300 km money-back return policy for retailer-model purchases, including dashboard-initiated return, pickup scheduling, and refund tracking.
Aftercare and maintenance pickup with service-type selection (routine service, repair, body work, AC, tires, other), photo evidence, cost estimate and digital approval, and real-time status from request to return.
Add-on car services purchasable from approved vendors: wash and detailing, tinting, registration renewal, paint protection, tire replacement, glass and windshield, and a car health check.
Dealer and showroom onboarding, inventory management, dealer dashboards, dealership pages with car counts and listings.
A B2B Dealer Auction Portal with timed auctions, live bidding with countdown, proxy/auto-bid, Buy Now, Make an Offer, watchlist, auction calendar, winning-bid invoicing, pickup scheduling, and arbitration.
Customer reviews and ratings, referral program, and value-tracker email updates.
Multi-language support (Arabic and English) with full right-to-left (RTL) and left-to-right (LTR) layouts.
Native mobile applications for iOS (Apple App Store), Android (Google Play) and Huawei (AppGallery), with personalized recommendations, offline favorites, in-app WhatsApp chat to customer support, biometric login, and camera capture for valuation.
A back-office admin portal for operations, sales agents, inspection teams, auction operators, delivery dispatchers, maintenance coordinators, finance officers, content editors, and super-administrators, with role-based access, a vehicle pipeline tracker, aging-based pricing, and a real-time analytics dashboard (units, revenue, gross profit per unit, inventory, days-to-sell, conversion funnel).
Out of scope for this SRS baseline: full peer-to-peer escrow payments between private buyers and sellers, B2B fleet leasing, and any cross-border GCC operations. These items may be added in future releases.

2. Overall Description
2.1 Product Perspective
The System is a new, self-contained product but interfaces with several external services: partner banks for loan origination and real-time pre-qualification, partner insurers for one-click policy activation, e-signature providers for digital contracts, payment gateways (KNET and Visa/Mastercard), SMS gateways for OTP and notifications, the WhatsApp Business API for automated order updates and customer-service chat, email service providers, the Ministry of Interior service portal (where electronic integration is permitted) for ownership-transfer status, mapping providers for vendor/driver locations and delivery tracking, and third-party inspection partners (such as Al Daman) and certified inspection garages.
Architecturally, the System is composed of:
Public Web Application — responsive single-page application accessible at the company domain, in Arabic and English.
Mobile Applications — native iOS app, native Android app, and a Huawei AppGallery build.
Admin Back-Office — web-only portal for internal users (operations, sales, inspection, auction operations, delivery dispatch, maintenance coordination, finance, content, super-admin).
Dealer Portal — web portal for car dealers and showrooms to manage their retail inventory and leads.
B2B Auction Portal — separately authenticated web portal for verified B2B dealers to participate in wholesale auctions, place proxy bids, and manage their auction account.
Driver/Delivery App — a lightweight mobile experience (PWA or native) used by delivery drivers for route, GPS broadcast, digital handover checklist and proof of delivery.
Backend Services — API gateway plus a set of domain services (Identity, Listings, Reservation, Checkout, Inspection, Financing, Insurance, Trade-In, Delivery, Returns, Maintenance, Auction, Services, Payments, Notifications, Content, Analytics).
Data Layer — primary relational database, search engine for catalog queries, object storage and CDN for media, cache layer, and a data warehouse for analytics.

2.4 Operating Environment
Web Frontend — Latest two major versions of Google Chrome, Safari, Microsoft Edge, Firefox, and Samsung Internet on desktop and mobile.
Mobile Apps — iOS 15+ (iPhone and iPad), Android 9+, Huawei devices running HMS Core.
Backend — Cloud-hosted (AWS, Azure or GCP) in a region with low latency to Kuwait (preferably Bahrain, UAE or Riyadh). Containerized using Docker, orchestrated with Kubernetes.
Database — Managed PostgreSQL (primary) and managed Redis (cache). Elasticsearch or OpenSearch for search.
Storage — Object storage (e.g. AWS S3 or Azure Blob) for car images and documents; CDN for global delivery (similar to the b-cdn.net pattern used by the reference platform).
Languages — Arabic (primary, RTL) and English (LTR), with locale selection persisted per user.
2.5 Design and Implementation Constraints
All pricing, financing calculators and dealer payouts shall be expressed in Kuwaiti Dinar (KWD) with three decimal places per local convention.
The application must fully support Arabic, including right-to-left layouts, Arabic numerals where requested, and locale-correct date formats.
All PII shall be stored within an approved cloud region and protected per CITRA Regulation No. 26 of 2024 (Kuwait Data Privacy Protection).
Payment processing must support KNET (Kuwait’s national debit network) in addition to Visa and Mastercard via a PCI-DSS certified gateway.
Loan-related screens and contracts must comply with Central Bank of Kuwait disclosure rules (APR, fees, total cost).
Ownership-transfer assistance must align with current MOI Traffic Department procedures.
Mobile apps must comply with Apple App Store, Google Play and Huawei AppGallery review guidelines.
Architecture must allow horizontal scaling of stateless services and zero-downtime deployments.
All source code must be version-controlled (Git); production deployments must follow CI/CD with automated tests.
2.6 Assumptions and Dependencies
Bank partners will provide stable APIs or, at minimum, secure SFTP/email pipelines for loan applications.
Inspection garages will provide trained personnel and a tablet/mobile-friendly UI to capture inspection data.
Verified vendors will agree to a service catalog and SLA terms before being onboarded.
The business will hold all required commercial licenses in Kuwait, including any used-car trading authorisation.
Adequate moderation staff will be available to review and approve self-service car listings within 24 hours.

6. Backend Specifications
6.1 Architectural Approach
The backend shall follow a modular service-oriented architecture. Initial implementation may be a modular monolith (one deployable, clearly separated modules) to minimize early operational overhead, with the ability to extract high-traffic domains (Listings, Search, Notifications) into independent services as scale demands.
API gateway (e.g. Kong, AWS API Gateway, or NGINX with rate-limit plugins) sits in front of all backend services.
Each module exposes versioned REST APIs (e.g. /v1/...) and a stable contract documented with OpenAPI.
Inter-module communication uses asynchronous messaging (e.g. Amazon SQS / RabbitMQ / Kafka) for non-blocking workflows (publish listing, send notification, generate report).
Background workers handle long-running jobs: report generation, image processing, indexing, finance partner submissions.
Search is powered by Elasticsearch / OpenSearch, kept up to date via outbox events.
Caching: Redis for hot reads (popular listings, configuration, session blacklists).
CDN: cached static assets and image variants served from edge.

6.6 Authentication, Authorization and Sessions
Access tokens shall be short-lived JSON Web Tokens (15 minutes) signed with RS256 or ES256.
Refresh tokens shall be opaque, rotated on use, stored hashed in the database with revocation support.
Authorization shall use role-based access control with per-resource scopes (e.g. dealer:listing:edit).
Sensitive admin endpoints shall require a TOTP-based second factor.
All authentication events shall be sent to a SIEM and reflected in the audit log.
6.7 File Storage and Media Pipeline
Uploads shall stream directly to object storage via pre-signed URLs.
Images shall be processed asynchronously: orientation, watermark, EXIF strip, AVIF/WebP variants at multiple widths (160, 320, 640, 1024, 1600).
Documents shall be encrypted at rest and accessible only via short-lived signed URLs.
Public images shall be served via CDN with appropriate cache headers and immutable filenames.
6.8 Background Jobs and Workflows
Image processing (per upload).
Search indexing (on listing create/update/delete).
Email/SMS/push delivery (with retries and backoff).
PDF inspection report generation.
Saved-search alert fan-out.
Listing-expiry sweeper (daily).
Bank-partner submission and status polling.
Daily KPI rollups and data-warehouse ETL.
6.9 Logging, Monitoring and Observability
Structured JSON logging from every service; correlation IDs propagated end-to-end.
Metrics: RED (Rate, Errors, Duration) per service; saturation metrics for queues and database.
Distributed tracing for at least the hot paths (browse, detail, offer, booking, payment).
SLO dashboards: 99.9% monthly availability target for the public web and APIs.
Alerting: PagerDuty / Opsgenie integration with severity-based escalation.
6.10 Backups and Disaster Recovery
Database backups: continuous WAL archiving plus daily snapshots, retained 35 days; quarterly restore drills.
Object storage versioning enabled; lifecycle rules for cost optimization.
RPO (Recovery Point Objective) ≤ 5 minutes; RTO (Recovery Time Objective) ≤ 1 hour for core services.
Disaster-recovery runbook reviewed semi-annually.

H. Reservation, Refund and Return-Policy Schedule
The following schedule summarizes baseline timeline rules; specific values are configurable in System settings and may be varied by promotion or regulation.
Item
Rule
Reservation Deposit Amount
KWD 100, fully refundable subject to the rules below.
Reservation Hold Duration
48 hours from successful deposit (configurable).
Auto-Refund on Hold Expiry
Full deposit refunded to the original instrument within 5 minutes of expiry.
Buyer-Initiated Cancellation Before Contract Signing
Full deposit refunded.
Buyer-Initiated Cancellation After Contract Signing, Before Delivery
Subject to a contract-cancellation fee per Terms; remainder refunded.
Return Window
3 days OR 300 km since delivery, whichever occurs first.
Return Within Policy Thresholds
Full refund to the original payment instrument, less only any explicitly disclosed re-stocking fee (where applicable).
Return Over Mileage Threshold
Per-km deduction applied per the policy schedule, with customer acknowledgment required before the refund proceeds.
Return With Damage
Damage repair-cost deduction itemized with photo evidence, with customer acknowledgment required before refund proceeds.
Refund Processing Time
Approved refunds dispatched to the original payment instrument within 5 business days of approval (excluding bank processing time).
